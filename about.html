Predicting Life Expectencies
- March 2024 -

Project Lead, Data Engineer


Conducted an in-depth analysis of a dataset sourced from the World Health Organization (WHO) encompassing global health statistics from 2000 to 2015. The dataset includes health indicators and other factors influencing life expectancy across various countries.

Data Processing and Analysis: > Cleaned and standardized data to ensure accuracy and consistency, facilitating effective analysis. > Implemented a Random Forest Regression model to uncover key patterns and predictive factors affecting life expectancy, focusing on variables such as lifestyle choices and environmental conditions. > Conducted extensive testing and performance evaluation, achieving an accuracy of 94.86% for the model's predictions on life expectancy and related mortality rates. > Utilized Python libraries such as Pandas for data manipulation, Scikit-learn for building and validating machine learning models, and Matplotlib for visualizing results. The analysis provided valuable insights into the determinants of life expectancy and mortality rates, contributing to a better understanding of public health trends globally.
